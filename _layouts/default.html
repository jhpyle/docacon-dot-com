Increase your docassemble skills

Docacon 2020 is a series of on-line Zoom sessions focused on hands-on training.

The beginner training on Friday morning is appropriate for people who have no experience or a little experience with docassemble.

The advanced training on Thursday morning is appropriate for people who are already developing guided interviews in docassemble.

Attendees can self-select into whichever training they think is appropriate.

In addition to these primary trainings, there are additional sessions in the afternoons on both days.

Registration cost: free. If you enjoy using open-source software and attending free conferences, consider making a donation to the Merlin Legal Open Source Foundation to help sustain this and other open-source projects.

All sessions will be held over Zoom. To get the URLs for joining a Zoom session, register. The links will be e-mailed to registrants prior to the conference. Join whichever sessions you wish.

The hands-on trainings will require you to run Zoom, Slack, and a web browser at the same time. If you have multiple devices, we recommend viewing Zoom in full screen mode on one device and using the other device for Slack and your web browser. If you have two monitors, you could run Zoom on your second monitor. The device on which you view Zoom should have a screen larger than 10 inches so that you can read text from a screen share.

Thursday, June 25

Advanced training

9:00 a.m. to 12:00 a.m. EDT

This hands-on training is for users with substantial experience using docassemble. If you are just getting started, the Beginner training on Friday will be more appropriate.

Topics will include: how docassemble logic works, overriding general questions with specific questions, review screens, and how to implement processes that divert from the normal interview logic.

Over the course of the morning there will be two Zoom sessions, with time in the middle to take a break from Zoom and do homework.

Screenshare your creation

13:00 to 13:45 EDT

This informal session will give you a chance to briefly share your screen and show off something you have created in docassemble. No preparation required, but you are encouraged to share something, regardless of your level of expertise!

Drop-in Q&A and troubleshooting

14:00 to 15:00 EDT

The docassemble community primarily uses Slack to ask questions and get help from peers with tricky situations. Let's try something similar over Zoom. Prepare some questions!

Drop-in Q&A and troubleshooting: far-away time zone edition

19:00 to 20:00 EDT

Docacon 2020 is scheduled for the USA/Eastern time zone because it was originally going to be held in Florida. This session will take place on Thursday evening, which is Friday morning in Australia. Bring your questions and problems!

Friday, June 26

Beginner training

9:00 to 12:00 EDT

This training is appropriate for people who have no experience or some experience with docassemble.

Topics will include: writing a "Hello, World" interview, writing questions, using different types of fields to gather information, working with checkboxes, using basic if/else logic, controlling question order, and assembling your first document.

Gathering groups of things

14:00 to 15:00 EDT

Docassemble lets you gather lists of objects, as well as dictionaries and sets. Using declarative "question" and "code" blocks, you can gather any arbitrary data structure. It can get complicated, though. This session will explain how group gathering works. Participants from both the Advanced and Beginner trainings are welcome.

Happy hour

16:00 to 17:00 EDT

Docacon will wrap up with Zoom social hour. Arrive whenever, leave whenever! Bring your own beverage.
